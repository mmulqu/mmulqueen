About Me

I'm a passionate GIS professional with 10 years of experience in spatial analysis, cartography, and geospatial data science. My journey in geography began with a fascination for maps and has evolved into expertise in applying machine learning techniques to solve complex spatial problems.

I hold a B.S. in Physics and Mathematics from SUNY Stony Brook, where I later specialized in geospatial sciences. My academic research focused on hyperspectral imagery and CNNs which sparked my interest in integrating deep learning with traditional GIS methodologies.

When I'm not analyzing spatial data or training models, you can find me hiking with my GPS unit, contributing to open-source geospatial projects, or teaching GIS workshops to help others discover the power of spatial thinking.
